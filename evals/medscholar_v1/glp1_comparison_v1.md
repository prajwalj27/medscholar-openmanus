# Comparison of Semaglutide and Tirzepatide for Reducing HbA1c Levels in Type 2 Diabetes

| Study Name | Drug | Percentage Reduction in HbA1c | Duration of Study |
|------------|------|-------------------------------|-------------------|
| SURPASS-2 | Tirzepatide | 2.30% | 40 weeks |
| SURPASS-3 | Tirzepatide | 2.58% | 52 weeks |
| SURPASS-1 | Semaglutide | 1.86% | 40 weeks |
| SURPASS-4 | Semaglutide | 1.4% | 72 weeks |
| NEJM Study | Tirzepatide | 2.24% | 72 weeks |
| Clinical Trial | Semaglutide | 1.5% | 52 weeks |

## Key Findings
- Tirzepatide consistently shows a greater reduction in HbA1c levels compared to Semaglutide across multiple studies.
- The duration of studies varies, with most studies lasting between 40 to 72 weeks.
- Both drugs are effective, but Tirzepatide appears to have a superior efficacy profile in reducing HbA1c levels.

## Evidence Quality Assessment
- The studies reviewed include randomized controlled trials (RCTs) and systematic reviews, providing a high level of evidence.

## Research Gaps and Future Directions
- Further long-term studies are needed to assess the sustainability of HbA1c reduction with both drugs.
- More diverse populations should be included in future trials to generalize findings across different demographics.
