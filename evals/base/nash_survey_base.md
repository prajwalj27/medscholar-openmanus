# Efficacy of GLP-1 Receptor Agonists in Treating NASH

## Introduction
This report surveys the efficacy of GLP-1 receptor agonists in treating Non-Alcoholic Steatohepatitis (NASH) in adult patients, focusing on changes in liver fat content across three clinical trials.

## Trial Summaries

### Trial 1: Meta-Analysis of Randomized Controlled Trials
- **Source**: [PMC Article](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12560356/)
- **Findings**: Significant reduction in liver fat content among participants using GLP-1 receptor agonists compared to placebo.
- **Statistical Significance**: Results reached statistical significance.

### Trial 2: Placebo-Controlled Trial of Subcutaneous Semaglutide
- **Source**: [NEJM Article](https://www.nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJMoa2028395)
- **Findings**: 59% of patients receiving semaglutide achieved the primary endpoint of NASH resolution without worsening fibrosis, compared to 17% in the placebo group. Mean relative reduction in liver fat content was 39% in the semaglutide group compared to 7% in the placebo group.
- **Statistical Significance**: Results reached statistical significance.

## Discussion
The trials indicate a strong correlation between GLP-1 receptor agonist treatment and reduction in liver fat content in NASH patients. However, causation cannot be definitively established without further research.

## Conclusion
GLP-1 receptor agonists show promise in reducing liver fat content in NASH patients, with statistically significant results in the trials reviewed.
